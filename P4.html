Early Life in Alpharetta

Griffin Sanders was raised in the vibrant city of Alpharetta, Georgia. Growing up in a close-knit community, Griffin developed a love for learning early on. He graduated high school with honors and remembers his freshman year vividly—particularly the HTML class that sparked his interest in web development. That moment planted a seed that eventually blossomed into his passion for computer science, leading him to pursue it as a college major.

Brotherhood and Leadership

During his time at Albany State University, Griffin joined the esteemed Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. His commitment and leadership skills quickly became evident. Within just a few years, he rose through the ranks and proudly served as chapter president. Through this role, Griffin honed his organizational and public speaking abilities while building a brotherhood that supported his academic and professional journey.

Family Inspiration

Griffin’s drive to succeed in technology was heavily influenced by his family. His father encouraged a strong work ethic and curiosity, while his cousin—currently a computer architect and help desk technician at a company called Wheels—showed him real-world examples of what a tech career could look like. Inspired by their achievements, Griffin aims to push the boundaries of innovation in computer science and contribute meaningfully to the tech industry.

What it means to be a computer science major

Being a computer science major is important because it equips individuals with the skills to solve complex problems, drive innovation, and shape the future of technology across all industries. From developing software and securing networks to advancing artificial intelligence and improving healthcare systems, computer scientists play a critical role in building the digital infrastructure of modern society. As the world becomes increasingly dependent on technology, a background in computer science opens doors to high-demand careers and empowers individuals to create impactful solutions that improve lives and transform the way we live and work. .

Computer science can be challenging due to its fast-paced evolution, steep learning curve, and the need for strong problem-solving skills. Students often face difficulties with abstract concepts like algorithms, data structures, and debugging complex code. Additionally, the pressure to constantly adapt to new technologies and tools can be overwhelming. However, these hardships build resilience and critical thinking, which are essential traits for success in the tech industry.

However....

"Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts."

Winston Churchill
  • High Demand & Job Security: Computer science skills are in high demand across nearly every industry, offering excellent job prospects and long-term career stability.
  • Innovation & Impact: It allows you to create technology that can solve real-world problems and positively impact millions of lives.
  • Versatility & Flexibility: With a computer science degree, you can work in various fields like AI, cybersecurity, software development, data science, and more.
